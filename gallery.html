Gallery

Explore the visual diversity of rocketry and propulsion — from model engines and launches to real-world artifacts and tech marvels captured through the lens.

AGM-76A Wind Tunnel Test Model
This model was used to study airflow and surface interactions for the AGM-76A missile prototype. The red tufts indicate flow patterns during wind tunnel testing. These tests are crucial in shaping real-world missile designs and improving flight control systems.

Missile Guidance and Reentry Vehicle Components This display reveals the internal chassis and electronic systems responsible for missile guidance and control. The cone on the right is part of a reentry vehicle used to deliver payloads through Earth’s atmosphere, protected by heat-resistant shielding.

Dipole Dispenser from Project Westford This spring-loaded mechanism was launched by the U.S. Army in 1963 to disperse thousands of wire antennas into Earth orbit, creating an artificial ionosphere to reflect radio signals. It was one of the first experiments in space-based communications.

Namba Rocket Payload Components These instruments were used to collect scientific data on atmospheric conditions, solar radiation, and cosmic particles. The variety of sensors and ejectable components helped advance our understanding of high-altitude physics.

Launch Vehicle Models (1:15 Scale) These precision-engineered models showcase the design variations of global rocket systems, including Delta IV and KSLV-II. Each rocket serves a unique role in launching satellites and deep space probes.

Ultraviolet and Atmospheric Payloads These devices were designed to be launched inside the nosecone of a research rocket, then deployed mid-flight. Instruments like these helped collect data on ozone levels, auroras, and upper-atmosphere chemistry.

Historic Warbirds Exhibit This hangar includes aircraft from WWII and the Cold War, including the B-29 Superfortress “Enola Gay.” Each plane represents a leap in aerospace technology and tactics.

Corona Reconnaissance Satellite A Cold War spy satellite used by the U.S. to photograph enemy military activity from space. Film was returned to Earth inside a capsule and caught mid-air by recovery aircraft.

ARCAS Sounding Rocket Payload & TETR Satellite Used for atmospheric research and training, these compact payloads carried telemetry and sensors to altitudes unreachable by balloons.

Space Shuttle Main Engines (RS-25) These three reusable engines powered NASA’s Space Shuttle into orbit. They burned liquid hydrogen and oxygen and could be restarted mid-flight.
