Visit Aerospace Museums

Explore real-world rocket and propulsion history at these aerospace museums.

Kennedy Space Center

Merritt Island, Florida

NASA's launch headquarters for Apollo, Shuttle, and Artemis missions.

Johnson Space Center

Houston, Texas

Mission Control and astronaut training for human spaceflight.

Marshall Space Flight Center

Huntsville, Alabama

Designed the Saturn V rocket; develops deep space propulsion.

Jet Propulsion Laboratory

Pasadena, California

Robotic exploration of the Solar System, Mars Rovers, Voyager.

Wallops Flight Facility

Wallops Island, Virginia

Launches for satellites, sounding rockets, and ISS resupply.

Goddard Space Flight Center

Greenbelt, Maryland

Earth observation satellites and Hubble Space Telescope operations.

Stennis Space Center

Hancock County, Mississippi

Main rocket engine testing facility for Artemis/SLS missions.

Ames Research Center

Moffett Field, California

Spacecraft design, supercomputing, astrobiology innovations.

National Air and Space Museum

Washington, D.C.

World’s largest collection of air and spacecraft; Apollo capsules.

Udvar-Hazy Center

Chantilly, Virginia

Massive exhibit featuring Shuttle Discovery and aviation icons.

Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum

New York City, New York

Home to Shuttle Enterprise, aboard an aircraft carrier on the Hudson River.

U.S. Space & Rocket Center

Huntsville, Alabama

Saturn V, Pathfinder Shuttle, Space Camp USA adventures!
